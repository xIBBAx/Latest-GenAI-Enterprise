# summary

**Badshah** (born Aditya Prateek Singh Sisodia on November 19, 1985) is an Indian rapper, singer, and music producer known for his contributions to Indian hip hop, Bollywood, and Punjabi music. [1][2][3][4] He gained widespread recognition for blending hip-hop, pop, and *desi* (local) influences in his music, creating a unique style that has resonated with a broad audience.[5][6] Badshah's rise from a "small-town Delhi boy" to a prominent figure in the Indian music scene is considered remarkable, and he is often credited as one of the first artists in India to embrace Desi Hip Hop.[7][8]
Badshah's career includes numerous Bollywood hits, such as "Saturday Saturday," "Kar Gayi Chull," and "Garmi," solidifying his presence in mainstream Indian music.[9][10][11][12] He has also collaborated with international artists like J Balvin and Tainy, with their trilingual anthem "Voodoo" gaining international attention and even being featured at an Apple Keynote event.[13][14][15][16] His debut album, *ONE*, released in 2018, showcased his versatility and included collaborations with leading Indian singers.[17] Beyond music, Badshah has ventured into acting, appearing in a film alongside Sonakshi Sinha, and is recognized as a fashion icon and cultural innovator.[18][19][20]
While Badshah has achieved significant success and recognition, including awards and nominations, he has also faced criticism.[21] Some listeners have expressed concerns that his recent lyrics are becoming overly complex, affecting their enjoyment of his newer music.[22] He also faced public criticism for comments made about global pop star Dua Lipa.[23]
Despite controversies, Badshah's impact on the Indian music industry and popular culture is undeniable. His work reflects the evolving landscape of content creation and consumption in the digital age, and he continues to be a prominent figure in the Indian entertainment industry, influencing music, television, movies, and fashion.[24][18] He is also set to become the first Punjabi rapper to debut at Paris Men's Fashion Week as a celebrity guest in June 2025 [18].

# Early Life and Education

Badshah, whose real name is Aditya Prateek Singh Sisodia [1], was born on November 19, 1985, in Delhi, India [2]. His father is from Haryana, and his mother is from Punjab [25][26]. She worked as a teacher [27]. He also has a younger sister named Aprajita Singh [27].
Badshah attended Bal Bharti Public School in Pitampura, Delhi [2][28]. He demonstrated an aptitude for mathematics, winning a National Olympiad gold medal [28]. He also scored highly on the CAT (Common Admission Test) [28]. Despite his early inclination towards traditional Indian music [29], he developed an interest in rap and hip-hop during his college years [29][30]. Before fully venturing into music, Badshah pursued civil engineering at PEC University in Chandigarh [29][31][32]. He even worked as a civil engineer for a period [32].

# Musical Career

Badshah developed a passion for music at a young age [29]. He was exposed to new music genres at PEC (Punjab Engineering College), which inspired him to begin writing rap as a hobby [33]. This hobby eventually turned into a profession [33]. Badshah's journey from a "small-town Delhi boy" to a sensation in the Indian rapping scene is considered remarkable [7].
Badshah's unique musical style blends hip-hop, pop, and "desi influences" [5]. Some critics have noted a distinction between Badshah's "Bollywood-ified club-rap" and the Indian rap underground [34]. His entry into Bollywood came with "Saturday Saturday," which became a popular party anthem [9]. He has since rapped in numerous Bollywood films [9], including "Abhi Toh Party Shuru Hui Hai" from *Khoobsurat* (2014), "Kar Gayi Chull" from *Kapoor and Sons*, "Garmi" from *Street Dancer 3D*, and "Naina" from *Crew* [10].
Badshah has also collaborated with international artists such as J Balvin and Tainy [10]. Their collaboration "Voodoo" was a trilingual anthem in Hindi, Spanish, and English [13] [14]. J Balvin stated that music connects people despite language barriers and praised Badshah and Tainy as tremendous artists [15] [16]. "Voodoo" was even featured at an Apple Keynote event [13].
Badshah is also set to become the first Punjabi rapper to debut at Paris Men's Fashion Week as a celebrity guest in June 2025 [18].

He is considered a prominent figure in the Indian music scene [3][4], and some consider him one of the first artists in India to embrace what is often called Desi Hip Hop [8]. His music incorporates elements of Punjabi gangster music, a genre that has gained popularity, particularly among artists from Punjab [37]. Badshah's influence extends beyond music into fashion [38][18], and he is seen as a cultural innovator [18]. # Discography

Badshah's discography includes both studio albums and numerous singles, often featuring collaborations with other artists [17][41][42]. His debut album, *ONE*, released in 2018, features 17 songs spanning various moods and includes collaborations with prominent singers like Aastha Gill [17]. Some of his popular tracks include "DJ Wale Babu," "Kar Gayi Chull," "Selfie le le re," "Kala Chashma," and "Mercy" [8]. He is also known for songs like "Saturday Saturday," which was featured in the soundtrack of a 2014 film, and "Abhi Toh Party Shuru Hui Hai," also featuring Aastha Gill [41][42].
