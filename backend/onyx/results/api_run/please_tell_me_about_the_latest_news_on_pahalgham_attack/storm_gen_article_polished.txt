# summary

The **Pahalgam attack** was a terrorist attack that occurred on April 22, 2025, in the resort town of Pahalgam, Jammu and Kashmir, India. The attack, which targeted tourists in the Baisaran Valley, resulted in the deaths of 24 to 28 people, including two foreign nationals, and is considered one of the deadliest in the region in recent times [1][2][3][4]. Dozens more were wounded in the attack [5].
Responsibility for the attack was claimed by The Resistance Front (TRF), described as a proxy for Lashkar-e-Taiba (LeT), a Pakistan-based organization [6][7][8]. Investigations suggest the involvement of Pakistani terrorists, leading to arrests of locals accused of aiding them [9][10][11]. LeT has been held responsible for a series of attacks, including the 2008 Mumbai attacks [8][12][13].
The attack was widely condemned internationally, including by the United Nations Security Council, and prompted widespread calls for the perpetrators to be brought to justice [5][14][15][16][17][18]. The Indian government responded with a multi-pronged strategy, including downgrading diplomatic ties with Pakistan and launching "Operation Sindoor," a military campaign targeting terrorist infrastructure [19][20][21][22][23].
The Pahalgam attack has had a significant impact on the region, leading to a sharp decline in tourism and raising concerns about the security situation in Jammu and Kashmir [24][25][26]. The incident reignited tensions between India and Pakistan and sparked political debate regarding security lapses and preventative measures [27][28][29][30].

# Overview

On April 22, 2025, a terrorist attack in the resort town of Pahalgam, Jammu and Kashmir, resulted in the deaths of 24 to 28 people, including two foreign nationals [1][2][3]. The attack, which targeted tourists in the Baisaran Valley, is considered one of the deadliest in the region in recent times [1][4]. Initial reports indicated that at least three dozen people were wounded [5].
The Resistance Front (TRF), described as a proxy for Lashkar-e-Taiba (LeT), claimed responsibility for the attack [6][7]. LeT itself is held responsible for a series of attacks, including the 2008 Mumbai attacks [8][12][13]. Investigations suggest the involvement of Pakistani terrorists in the Pahalgam attack [9][10]. The National Investigation Agency (NIA) has arrested locals for aiding Pakistani LeT terrorists involved in the attack [11].
The attack has been widely condemned, with many calling for the perpetrators to be brought to justice [14][15][16]. Some reports characterized the attack as a targeted assault on tourists [12]. The incident has raised concerns about the safety of tourists in the region and has led to discussions about the security situation in Jammu and Kashmir [24].

# Background

The Pahalgam attack, which occurred in 2025, is described as a terrorist attack in the Indian-administered Jammu and Kashmir [31]. The attack is considered the most horrifying in the region since the 2019 Pulwama attack [32]. The attack targeted tourists near Pahalgam [12] [33] [31]. The Resistance Front (TRF), a splinter group linked to Lashkar-e-Taiba, has been implicated in orchestrating terror attacks in Kashmir, targeting both civilians and security forces [12] [34]. The attack has led to heightened security measures and condemnation [35] [36] [6]. The region of Jammu and Kashmir has a long history of territorial dispute between India and Pakistan [37].

# The Attack

On April 22, 2025, a terror attack occurred in Pahalgam, resulting in a significant loss of life [3] [38]. The attack took place around 2:30 pm or 3 pm [39]. Initial reports indicate that 28 individuals were killed in what is described as a heinous massacre [3]. The Resistance Front (TRF), a shadow outfit of Lashkar-e-Taiba (LeT), a Pakistan-based organization, has claimed responsibility for the attack [7] [40]. The incident has drawn nationwide outrage and has led to intensified counter-terrorism operations in Jammu and Kashmir [41]. Pahalgam has witnessed militant violence in the past, including grenade attacks and assaults on tourist buses [42]. The April 22 attack is considered one of the largest attacks in the area [3].

# Aftermath

The Pahalgam attack on April 22, 2025, which resulted in the deaths of at least 26 people [43][3][44], including tourists [45][44], triggered widespread condemnation and a multi-pronged response from India and the international community. Many more were wounded, some seriously [5].
Following the attack, Prime Minister Narendra Modi conveyed the nation's support to the victims and their families [43]. The Ministry of External Affairs (MEA) held a special briefing [19]. The United Nations Security Council (UNSC) [17][18] and Secretary-General Ant√≥nio Guterres [5] strongly condemned the attack, emphasizing the unacceptability of targeting civilians [5]. Saudi Arabia's Ministry of Foreign Affairs also denounced the attack [46].
India responded with a five-pronged strategy [19][20], including downgrading diplomatic ties with Pakistan [20]. The Attari check post was shut, and Pakistani nationals were instructed to return by May 1 [19]. Operation Sindoor was launched as a decisive military campaign targeting terrorist infrastructure [21][22][23].
The National Investigation Agency (NIA) arrested individuals accused of providing safe harbor to the terrorists involved [47][11][48]. An investigation involving questioning over 2800 people and detaining 150 was conducted [49].
The attack also sparked political reactions, with some attributing the incident to security lapses [27]. A political representative from a regional party commented on a recent terrorist attack in a tourist area, attributing the incident to significant security [27]. The government faced questions regarding preventative measures [50].
The attack has had a significant impact on the local community, with residents facing mounting livelihood losses [51].

# Impact

The terrorist attack in Pahalgam on April 22, 2025, had a wide-ranging impact, affecting tourism, security perceptions, and regional politics. The attack, which resulted in the deaths of 26 people, primarily tourists [52][53], has been described as the biggest one targeting tourists in recent years [52].
The immediate aftermath saw a sharp decline in tourism in Pahalgam [25]. The occupancy rate in over 1500 hotels plummeted [26], impacting the livelihoods of those dependent on the tourism industry, including apricot farmers in Udhampur [25]. The attack is viewed as a targeted assault on tourists [12][45], potentially signaling a shift in militant tactics after years of avoiding such attacks [54].
Politically, the attack reignited tensions between India and Pakistan [28], with some attributing the incident to security failures [27][29][30]. Congress president Mallikarjun Kharge condemned the attack and called for the perpetrators to be punished [16]. The United Nations Security Council also issued a statement condemning the terrorist attack [17]. The Indian government responded with "Operation Sindoor," a military campaign aimed at neutralizing terrorist infrastructure [21][23]. The attack also prompted diplomatic outreach from the Union government [55].
