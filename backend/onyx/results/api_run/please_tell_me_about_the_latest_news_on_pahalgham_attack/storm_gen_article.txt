# Overview

On April 22, 2025, a terrorist attack in the resort town of Pahalgam, Jammu and Kashmir, resulted in the deaths of 24 to 28 people, including two foreign nationals [1][2][3]. The attack, which targeted tourists in the Baisaran Valley, is considered one of the deadliest in the region in recent times [1][4]. Initial reports indicated that at least three dozen people were wounded [5].
The Resistance Front (TRF), described as a proxy for Lashkar-e-Taiba (LeT), claimed responsibility for the attack [6][7]. LeT itself is held responsible for a series of attacks, including the 2008 Mumbai attacks [8][9][10]. Investigations suggest the involvement of Pakistani terrorists in the Pahalgam attack [11][12]. The National Investigation Agency (NIA) has arrested locals for aiding Pakistani LeT terrorists involved in the attack [13].
The attack has been widely condemned, with many calling for the perpetrators to be brought to justice [14][15][16]. Some reports characterized the attack as a targeted assault on tourists [9]. The incident has raised concerns about the safety of tourists in the region and has led to discussions about the security situation in Jammu and Kashmir [17].

# Background

The Pahalgam attack, which occurred in 2025, is described as a terrorist attack in the Indian-administered Jammu and Kashmir [18]. The attack is considered the most horrifying in the region since the 2019 Pulwama attack [19]. The attack targeted tourists near Pahalgam [9] [20] [18]. The Resistance Front (TRF), a splinter group linked to Lashkar-e-Taiba, has been implicated in orchestrating terror attacks in Kashmir, targeting both civilians and security forces [9] [21]. The attack has led to heightened security measures and condemnation [22] [23] [6]. The region of Jammu and Kashmir has a long history of territorial dispute between India and Pakistan [24].

# The Attack

On April 22, 2025, a terror attack occurred in Pahalgam, resulting in a significant loss of life [3] [25]. The attack took place around 2:30 pm or 3 pm [26]. Initial reports indicate that 28 individuals were killed in what is described as a heinous massacre [3]. The Resistance Front (TRF), a shadow outfit of Lashkar-e-Taiba (LeT), a Pakistan-based organization, has claimed responsibility for the attack [7] [27]. The incident has drawn nationwide outrage and has led to intensified counter-terrorism operations in Jammu and Kashmir [28]. Pahalgam has witnessed militant violence in the past, including grenade attacks and assaults on tourist buses [29]. The April 22 attack is considered one of the largest attacks in the area [3].

# Aftermath

The Pahalgam attack on April 22, 2025, which resulted in the deaths of at least 26 people [30][3][31], including tourists [32][31], triggered widespread condemnation and a multi-pronged response from India and the international community. Many more were wounded, some seriously [5].
Following the attack, Prime Minister Narendra Modi conveyed the nation's support to the victims and their families [30]. The Ministry of External Affairs (MEA) held a special briefing [33]. The United Nations Security Council (UNSC) [34][35] and Secretary-General Ant√≥nio Guterres [5] strongly condemned the attack, emphasizing the unacceptability of targeting civilians [5]. Saudi Arabia's Ministry of Foreign Affairs also denounced the attack [36].
India responded with a five-pronged strategy [33][37], including downgrading diplomatic ties with Pakistan [37]. The Attari check post was shut, and Pakistani nationals were instructed to return by May 1 [33]. Operation Sindoor was launched as a decisive military campaign targeting terrorist infrastructure [38][39][40].
The National Investigation Agency (NIA) arrested individuals accused of providing safe harbor to the terrorists involved [41][13][42]. An investigation involving questioning over 2800 people and detaining 150 was conducted [43].
The attack also sparked political reactions, with some attributing the incident to security lapses [44]. A political representative from a regional party commented on a recent terrorist attack in a tourist area, attributing the incident to significant security [44]. The government faced questions regarding preventative measures [45].
The attack has had a significant impact on the local community, with residents facing mounting livelihood losses [46].

# Impact

The terrorist attack in Pahalgam on April 22, 2025, had a wide-ranging impact, affecting tourism, security perceptions, and regional politics. The attack, which resulted in the deaths of 26 people, primarily tourists [47][48], has been described as the biggest one targeting tourists in recent years [47].
The immediate aftermath saw a sharp decline in tourism in Pahalgam [49]. The occupancy rate in over 1500 hotels plummeted [50], impacting the livelihoods of those dependent on the tourism industry, including apricot farmers in Udhampur [49]. The attack is viewed as a targeted assault on tourists [9][32], potentially signaling a shift in militant tactics after years of avoiding such attacks [51].
Politically, the attack reignited tensions between India and Pakistan [52], with some attributing the incident to security failures [44][53][54]. Congress president Mallikarjun Kharge condemned the attack and called for the perpetrators to be punished [16]. The United Nations Security Council also issued a statement condemning the terrorist attack [34]. The Indian government responded with "Operation Sindoor," a military campaign aimed at neutralizing terrorist infrastructure [38][40]. The attack also prompted diplomatic outreach from the Union government [55].
