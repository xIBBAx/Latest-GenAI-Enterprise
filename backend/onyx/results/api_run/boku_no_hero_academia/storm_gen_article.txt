# Overview

*My Hero Academia* (Japanese: 僕のヒーローアカデミア, Hepburn: *Boku no Hīrō Akademia*) is a Japanese superhero manga series written and illustrated by Kōhei Horikoshi [1]. The series is set in a world where approximately 80% of the human population possesses superpowers, known as "Quirks" (個性, *Kosei*) [2][3][4]. These Quirks are generally unique to each user and are classified into multiple categories [5][6]. The story follows Izuku Midoriya, a boy born without a Quirk, but who dreams of becoming a hero [2][1].
The manga began its serialization in *Weekly Shōnen Jump* on July 7, 2014, and concluded on August 5, 2024, after a ten-year run spanning 430 chapters [7][8]. *Weekly Shōnen Jump* is a popular manga magazine that publishes new chapters of various manga series every week [9][10][11].
The series explores themes of friendship, perseverance, and the true meaning of heroism [12]. It also touches upon the idea that hard work and persistence can sometimes overcome natural talent [13][14][15]. The narrative focuses on Izuku Midoriya's journey to become the best hero, encouraging others to do the same, regardless of their shortcomings [16]. The relationship between Izuku Midoriya and All Might forms a central "vertical axis" of the story [17].

# Plot

*My Hero Academia* is set in a world where roughly 80% of the population possesses superpowers, known as "Quirks" [4]. The series follows Izuku Midoriya, a young boy born without a Quirk, but who dreams of becoming a hero [1].
The story begins with Izuku's admiration for heroes, especially All Might, the world's greatest hero [18]. Despite being Quirkless, Izuku's heroic spirit and determination impress All Might, who chooses Izuku as his successor, bestowing upon him his Quirk, "One For All" [19]. This Quirk grants Izuku immense strength, but he must learn to control it to prevent injuring himself [19].
Izuku enrolls in U.A. High School, a prestigious academy for aspiring heroes [20]. There, he befriends classmates like Katsuki Bakugo, his childhood friend and rival [21][18], and Ochaco Uraraka, developing his skills and learning what it truly means to be a hero [12]. The students of U.A. High School undergo rigorous training, including a school trip to a training camp [22], to hone their abilities and combat villains [20].
As the series progresses, the stakes escalate, with the heroes facing increasingly powerful villains and organizations [23]. The conflict between the heroes and villains intensifies, leading to climactic battles [20]. The story explores themes of friendship, perseverance, and the true meaning of heroism [12]. The protagonist's journey reveals the author's perspective on crucial life questions [24].

# Characters

## Protagonists

*   **Izuku Midoriya (Deku):** The main protagonist of the series, Izuku Midoriya, also known as Deku, is a student who strives to become the No. 1 Pro Hero, inheriting this goal from All Might [28] [29]. Initially born without a Quirk (superpower), he later gains the powerful Quirk "One For All" [30]. Midoriya's journey focuses on his hard work and dedication to achieving his dream [31] [15] [13]. He is often seen as an underdog rising through the ranks [32].
*   **Katsuki Bakugo:** A childhood friend of Izuku Midoriya, Bakugo possesses a powerful Quirk and is portrayed as intense and competitive [27] [18] [33]. He consistently ranks high in popularity polls [34] [35]. Some argue that Bakugo, with his ability to enhance his Quirk, is stronger than Midoriya in terms of raw ability [19].

## Supporting Characters

*   **Tenya Ida:** Another classmate, known for his seriousness and adherence to rules [37]. He is also known by his hero name "Ingenium" [36].
*   **Shoto Todoroki:** A student with a powerful Quirk, often ranked among the most popular characters [38] [35].

# Anime Adaptation

*My Hero Academia* was adapted into an anime television series produced by Bones [42]. The series is based on the manga of the same name by Kōhei Horikoshi [42]. The anime adapts the storyline of Izuku Midoriya, a boy who dreams of becoming a superhero in a world where 80% of the population possesses superpowers, known as "Quirks" [4][6][3]. After being granted a Quirk by the world's strongest hero, All Might, Midoriya enrolls in a prestigious hero academy to learn what it truly means to be a hero [43][44].
The first season of the anime premiered with thirteen episodes [45]. The animation quality has been a topic of discussion among fans, with some noting improvements after the first season [46], while others have expressed concerns about a decline in quality in later seasons [47].
The music for the *My Hero Academia* anime is composed and arranged by Yuki Hayashi [48][49][50][51][52]. Hayashi's work on the series has been well-received [53][54]. The opening themes of the anime have also garnered attention, with "No. 1" by DISH being the eighth opening theme and the first of Season 5 [55].
Some episodes are anime original, expanding on the manga storyline [56]. For example, one episode was created to set up the movie [56]. The anime series generally follows the plot of the manga, adapting key events and battles [20].

# Manga

*My Hero Academia* began as a manga series written and illustrated by Kōhei Horikoshi [57]. It is published in *Weekly Shōnen Jump*, a manga magazine that releases new chapters every Monday [9][10]. The first issue of *Weekly Shōnen Jump* was released on August 1, 1968, making it one of the longest-running manga magazines [10]. Horikoshi's artistic style evolved between the first and second chapters of the manga, becoming less "rubbery" [58].
Horikoshi has cited *Dragon Ball*, *One Piece*, and *Naruto* as some of his favorite manga series and influences from his childhood [39][59]. He also drew inspiration from Yuko Osada [ja] [39]. While not directly based on any pre-existing work, the series draws inspiration from various superhero and *shonen* manga genres [57].

# Themes and Analysis

*My Hero Academia* explores several prominent themes, including heroism, self-sacrifice, perseverance, friendship, and the importance of hard work [12] [29] [61]. The series delves into the true meaning of heroism [12], particularly through the journey of its main character, Izuku Midoriya [29] [61] [28].
A central theme revolves around the contrast between innate talent and dedicated effort [15] [13] [31] [62] [63]. The series suggests that while talent can be an advantage [62], hard work is essential for realizing one's full potential [13] [31]. This is exemplified by characters who strive to overcome their shortcomings and achieve greatness through diligence and determination [16] [64].
The narrative also examines the impact of quirks (superhuman abilities) on personality [65] [5] [6] [3]. The series suggests a connection between a character's abilities and their inherent traits [65] [66].

# Media Impact

*Boku no Hero Academia* has achieved significant media impact, contributing to the increased popularity of manga and anime in recent years [67]. Online communities and forums provide spaces for fans to discuss the series, share merchandise, and seek recommendations [69]. The series' narrative shift towards "end-game stakes" has been noted, with some observing a greater emphasis on plot development compared to character-driven moments [23]. The series also explores themes of hard work and perseverance, suggesting that dedication can sometimes overcome innate talent [14], and that talent is only useful when paired with hard work [15][13][31].
